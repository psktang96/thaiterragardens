Golden Shower Tree — Thailand's National Flower

Plant GuideJanuary 2026By Thai Terra Gardens
← Back to Blog

When the Golden Shower Tree blooms, it transforms the entire landscape around it. Cascading racemes of brilliant yellow flowers drape from every branch, creating a curtain of gold that is impossible to ignore. It is Thailand's national flower — and one of the most spectacular ornamental trees in the world.

Quick Facts

Botanical NameCassia fistula
Common NameGolden Shower Tree, Indian Laburnum, Ton Khun (Thai)
FamilyFabaceae (Leguminosae)
OriginIndian subcontinent; naturalised across Southeast Asia
Height10 – 20 metres at maturity
Bloom SeasonMarch – June (peak: April–May in Thailand)
National SymbolNational flower of Thailand; also Sri Lanka's national tree

A Tree of Cultural Significance

In Thailand, Cassia fistula — locally known as "Ton Khun" or "Ratchaphruek" — is far more than just a beautiful tree. It is the national flower of Thailand, deeply embedded in cultural and religious life. The golden colour of its blooms symbolises the monarchy and the spirit of the Thai nation. Every April during the Songkran festival, Thai streets lined with blooming Golden Shower Trees create breathtaking corridors of gold.

The tree is also sacred in Hindu and Buddhist traditions. In India, it is associated with the sun god Surya, and in Ayurvedic medicine, various parts of the tree — bark, leaves, pods, and roots — have been used for their medicinal properties for centuries.

The Blooming Spectacle

What makes the Golden Shower Tree so remarkable is the drama of its flowering. The tree is semi-deciduous — it sheds most of its leaves just before flowering, which means that when the blooms emerge, they are displayed against bare branches with virtually nothing to compete with them. Long, pendulous racemes of bright yellow flowers can reach 30–40cm in length, hanging down in dense clusters that give the tree its "golden shower" name.

A fully mature Golden Shower Tree in peak bloom is one of the most photographed trees in Asia. It makes an extraordinary focal point for parks, roadsides, hotel entrances, corporate campuses, and estate gardens. The spectacle typically lasts 2–4 weeks and is followed by the emergence of fresh new green foliage.

Growing Conditions

Cassia fistula is a robust and adaptable tree, well-suited to tropical and subtropical climates. Once established, it is fairly drought-tolerant and requires minimal intervention. In its preferred climate, it grows rapidly and begins blooming within 3–5 years from planting.

  • Sunlight: Full sun; produces poor flowering in shade
  • Soil: Adaptable; tolerates clay, loam, and sandy soils with reasonable drainage
  • Water: Moderate; drought-tolerant once established; do not waterlog
  • Temperature: Tropical to subtropical; sensitive to prolonged frost
  • Growth Rate: Fast (1–1.5m per year in good conditions)
  • Pruning: Light shaping after flowering to maintain form; avoid heavy cutting

Landscape Applications

The Golden Shower Tree is highly versatile in landscape design. Its moderate size (typically 10–15m in cultivation), rounded crown, and spectacular flowering make it suitable for:

  • Avenue and boulevard planting — creates spectacular "golden tunnel" effect
  • Hotel and resort entrance plantings
  • Public parks and botanical gardens
  • Private estate gardens as specimen trees
  • Urban streetscaping in tropical cities
  • Golf course landscaping
